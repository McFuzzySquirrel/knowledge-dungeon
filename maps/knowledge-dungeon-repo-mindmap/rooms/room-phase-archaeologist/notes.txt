# Archaeologist Phase

## The Goal

In Archaeologist phase, you **review and consolidate** what you've learned. You're the scholar revisiting knowledge to ensure long-term retention.

## What You Do

1. **Access artifacts** — All your cleared rooms are now reviewable
2. **Answer prompts** — Self-check questions about each topic
3. **Mark as reviewed** — Confirm you've mentally rehearsed the material
4. **Track streaks** — Build review streaks (3+ in a row = bonus)
5. **Unlock mastery** — Complete all reviews to finish the subject

## Self-Check Prompts

For each artifact, you get questions like:

- "What would you say is the core concept of {topic}?"
- "Where does this fit in the broader {subject} landscape?"
- "How would you explain this to someone unfamiliar with {topic}?"
- "What's the design philosophy behind this?"

These prompts are **open-ended** and **self-graded**. There's no right/wrong—you decide if you've retained the material.

## Review Mechanics

### The Streak System
- Review 3+ rooms consecutively without failing → unlock streak bonus
- Streak bonus = extra XP toward next rank
- Encourages **marathon study sessions** but not required

### Revalidation Option
- Unsure if you've learned something? Trigger revalidation
- Re-read your notes, re-answer the prompt, try again
- No penalty for revalidation, just reinforcement

### Mastery Unlock
- Complete **all rooms** with at least 1 review pass each
- Phase state becomes **SubjectMastered**
- Unlock final badges

## Why This Phase?

Learning science shows that **spacing** (reviewing material over time) improves retention. Archaeologist phase isn't a final exam—it's deliberate practice.

## Design Insight

Archaeologist phase treats learning as **iterative and non-linear**. You don't cram all reviews at once; you can return anytime. The streak system gamifies spaced repetition, turning retention practice into a satisfying challenge.
