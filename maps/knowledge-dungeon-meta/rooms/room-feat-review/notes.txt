# Review Mechanics

## The Review Loop

In Archaeologist phase, your job is to **revisit and consolidate** what you've learned:

1. **Select artifact** — Choose a cleared room from your list
2. **Read notes** — Re-read what you wrote in Scribe phase
3. **Answer prompts** — Self-check questions about the topic
4. **Mark reviewed** — Confirm you've mentally rehearsed

Repeat until all rooms are reviewed.

## Self-Check Prompts

Prompts vary by topic but follow a pattern:

- **What is it?** — "What would you say is the core concept of X?"
- **Where does it fit?** — "How does X relate to the broader subject?"
- **How do you use it?** — "When would you use X? Give an example."
- **Why is it designed that way?** — "What's the philosophy behind X?"

These are **open-ended and self-graded**. No "right answer"—you decide if you've retained the material.

## Streak System

Review streaks encourage **marathon study**:

- Clear 3+ rooms *in one session* → unlock streak bonus
- Streak bonus = extra XP toward next rank
- Motivates concentrated review periods

Streaks reset if you:
- Stop reviewing for the day
- Close the app
- Switch subjects

## Revalidation

Unsure about a topic? Revalidate:

- Open artifact
- Re-read notes carefully
- Answer prompts again
- If satisfied, mark reviewed

**No penalty** for revalidation—it's encouraged as reinforcement.

## Multiple Passes

You can review the same artifact multiple times:

- First pass: consolidate initial learning
- Second pass: weeks later, refresh memory
- Third pass: before an exam, final review

Each pass is tracked in `reviewPassCount` in room metadata.

## Mastery & Finality

Mastery requires:

- All rooms reviewed at least once
- Clear evidence you've thought deeply about each topic
- Phase state becomes `SubjectMastered`

After mastery, you can:
- Continue reviewing for reinforcement
- Create new subjects on related topics
- Export the subject as a completed portfolio

## Design Insight

Review mechanics are **non-linear and forgiving**. Unlike traditional exams, there's no "final chance"—you can review indefinitely. This aligns with learning science: **spaced repetition and active recall** are the best retention strategies, and there's no time limit on learning.
