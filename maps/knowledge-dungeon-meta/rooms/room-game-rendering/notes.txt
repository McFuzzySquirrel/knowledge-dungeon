# Dungeon Rendering & Navigation

## Phaser Canvas

The dungeon is rendered in a Phaser Canvas element:

- **Resolution** — Typically 1200x700 pixels
- **Camera** — Follows the player, shows nearby rooms
- **Layers** — Background tiles, room objects, player, effects

## Room Layout

Rooms are arranged in a **force-directed graph** layout:

- Rooms repel each other (avoid overlap)
- Edges pull connected rooms closer (hierarchy)
- Converges to a stable layout that reflects the topic structure

This layout is calculated once when the dungeon loads, then stored.

## Room Visuals

Each room is a **tile/node** containing:

- **Name** — Topic title
- **State indicator** — Color or icon showing if room is cleared, locked, etc.
- **Collision area** — Invisible polygon for interaction

### Room States (Visual):
- **Uncreated** — Grayed out, not yet in the map
- **Created** — Normal, not yet entered
- **Visited** — Slightly highlighted
- **NotesDrafted** — Darker, work in progress
- **EncounterDefeated** — Bright, cleared
- **ArtifactCollected** — Gold highlight

## Player Character

The player is a simple sprite:

- Controlled by WASD or arrow keys
- Moves smoothly with animation
- Has collision detection (can't walk through walls)
- Camera follows the player

## Navigation

### Keyboard
- `W A S D` — Move up/left/down/right
- `E` — Interact with current room
- `?` — Toggle help

### Touch (mobile/tablet)
- On-screen D-pad buttons
- Interact button

### Minimap
- Small overview in corner
- Shows all rooms and player position
- Click to jump to a room (Creator phase)

## Performance

The renderer uses:
- **Sprite pooling** — Reuse objects instead of creating new ones
- **Layer visibility** — Hide distant rooms
- **Canvas batching** — Group draw calls for efficiency
- **Lazy loading** — Load assets on demand

For typical subjects (10–100 rooms), performance is smooth on modern hardware.

## Design Insight

The dungeon visualization is **intentionally simple**. No fancy 3D graphics or particle effects. The goal is clarity: your knowledge structure should be immediately understandable by looking at the map.
